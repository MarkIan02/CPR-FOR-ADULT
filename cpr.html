How to Do CPR?

Step #1

In the event that the individual is not breathing, it is important to place one hand over the other and position them in the center of the person's chest, directly below their nipples. If you are assisting a child up to the age of 8, use one hand and position it just above the bottom of their breastbone. Remember, acting quickly and confidently can make a significant difference in saving a life.

How to Do CPR?

Step #2

With the force of their body weight, they should push their hands down firmly in the center of the person's chest. Using the heel of their hand or the area just before their wrist, they must keep their arms straight.

How to Do CPR?

Step #3

The individual should continue to push on the person's chest (chest compressions) at a rate of 100 to 120 times per minute, ensuring each compression goes down 2 inches (approximately the height or short side of a credit card). It is crucial to allow the chest to fully rise back up between each compression. Keep up the effort and maintain a steady rhythm to effectively perform CPR.

Step #4

Individuals with CPR training are able to confidently pause compressions in order to administer two mouth-to-mouth rescue breaths for every 30 compressions, which typically takes about 20 seconds. This technique is crucial in providing effective and life-saving care during an emergency situation.

Step #5

One should continue performing chest compressions and administering rescue breaths in a cycle until the individual is revived or additional assistance arrives. It is crucial to persist with these life-saving actions to increase the chances of a successful outcome.
